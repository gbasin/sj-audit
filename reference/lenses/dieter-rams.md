# Lens: Dieter Rams — Ten Principles for Good Design

The strategic-judgment lens. Less about interaction mechanics than about whether the product,
*as a whole*, is honest, restrained, and worth its complexity. Pairs well with Nielsen:
Nielsen gives tactical usability, Rams gives the step-back design verdict that catches
gratuitous animation, visual noise, and features that exist for the roadmap rather than the user.

## The 10 principles — judge the product against each

1. **Good design is innovative** — does it advance, or just clone a competitor? (Where does
   this feel like a thin Slack clone vs. where does its real identity show?)
2. **Good design makes a product useful** — emphasizes usefulness, discards anything that detracts.
3. **Good design is aesthetic** — well-executed beauty is part of usefulness; well-being.
4. **Good design makes a product understandable** — it clarifies structure; at best, self-explanatory.
5. **Good design is unobtrusive** — neutral and restrained, leaving room for the user; tools, not decoration.
6. **Good design is honest** — doesn't make a product seem more innovative/powerful/valuable
   than it is; doesn't manipulate with promises it can't keep. (Dead affordances and
   unconfigured features dangled as real features fail here.)
7. **Good design is long-lasting** — avoids being fashionable, never antiquated.
8. **Good design is thorough down to the last detail** — nothing arbitrary; care and accuracy
   are respect for the user. (Casing inconsistencies, stray drafts, misaligned empty states.)
9. **Good design is environmentally friendly** — conserves resources; minimizes waste
   (perf, payload, attention).
10. **Good design is as little design as possible** — less, but better. Concentrate on the
    essential; back to purity, back to simplicity.

## How to critique through this lens

Step back from individual screens and ask: is this honest? Is it restrained? Is it thorough?
What exists for the roadmap rather than the user? What noise competes with the essential?
The strongest Rams findings are about **subtraction and honesty**, not micro-usability.

## Verdict style

Calm, principled, evaluative — the voice of someone judging craft. Example:
- "**[P6 Honest · P8 Thorough]** A prominent call button that answers 'Calls aren't
  available' presents an unbuilt feature as a finished one. Honest design either ships it or
  removes the affordance — it does not dangle it."

## Severity mapping

- **P0** — dishonest or broken at the level of the whole product promise.
- **P1** — meaningful violation of usefulness/understandability/restraint.
- **P2** — thoroughness/detail lapses that erode trust in the craft.
- **P3** — refinements toward "less, but better".
